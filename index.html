Hospital - stroke

“The very first requirement in a hospital is that it should do the sick no harm.”

An ischemic stroke is defined as a reduction in blood supply or no blood supply to the brain or part of the brain. This results in decreased brain cell perfusion and reduced oxygen supply to the brain, leading to the death of brain cells.

The symptoms of ischemic stroke are paralysis, face numbness, slurred speech, loss of or blurred vision and trouble walking.

This is a medical emergency condition and needs to be treated immediately.
